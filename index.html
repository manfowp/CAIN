Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 2026

WILD COMPANY (referred to as "the Application") provides a high-stakes multiplayer horror experience. Your privacy is paramount. This document outlines our data practices to ensure transparency for our users and platform partners.

1. Information Collection and Use

WILD COMPANY is designed with a "Privacy by Default" architecture. We do not collect, store, or transmit any Personally Identifiable Information (PII) to external servers. This includes names, email addresses, location data, or contact lists. We do not require account registration to access the core gameplay loop.

2. Technical and Gameplay Data

To provide a functional multiplayer experience, the Application utilizes platform-provided infrastructure (such as Meta Quest or Steamworks). These platforms may collect anonymized hardware identifiers and crash logs to ensure Application stability. Any gameplay progress, scrap counts, and unlocked items are stored locally on your device or within the platform’s secure cloud-save system.

3. Voice and Communications

The Application features real-time, proximity-based voice chat. This communication is processed locally and routed via peer-to-peer networking or platform-hosted relays. We do not record, store, or monitor your voice communications.

4. Third-Party Services

While the Application itself does not collect data, it operates within third-party ecosystems. Your data interactions with these platforms are governed by their respective privacy policies. We encourage users to review the privacy settings of their gaming hardware provider.

5. Data Security

Since we do not collect personal data, there is no personal user information stored on our servers to breach. We recommend that users maintain the security of their platform accounts to protect their local save data and achievements.

6. Compliance

This policy is designed to comply with global privacy standards, including GDPR and CCPA, by ensuring that no personal data is harvested from the user during the operation of the Application.
